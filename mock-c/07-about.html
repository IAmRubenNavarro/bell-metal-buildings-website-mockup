About Bell Metal Buildings

We're more than a building company. We're a steel-town factory run by people whose grandfathers worked the same floor.

American values · Legendary founder

A factory you can call by name.

In 1962, William "Bell" Larkin opened a small fabrication shop in Aliquippa, Pennsylvania — a Beaver County town built on Jones & Laughlin steel. He'd spent the previous decade welding I-beams for Bethlehem, and he was sure that a smaller factory, run with his own hands, could turn out a better pre-engineered building than the volume shops of the day.

Sixty-four years later, three generations of Larkins still run the floor. The principles haven't changed: heavier gauge than the spec calls for, stamped engineering signed by a P.E. who actually walked your jurisdiction, and a project manager who picks up the phone after the kit ships.

What's grown is the work. Bell ships kits to all 48 contiguous states. We've stamped buildings for hangars in Alaska, mini-storage in Texas, barndominiums in Tennessee, and a riding arena that sits on the same hillside Bell's first metal Quonset was raised on in '64.

Still, the answer to "who builds these?" is the same as it was the day Bell opened: we do. In Aliquippa.

Bell's quality lives on

A building you'll still be standing under in 2080.

Bell's first sale was a 30×60 metal Quonset for a Beaver Falls farmer in 1962. It's still standing. The roof has been re-coated twice. The frame has never been touched. That's the standard we still hold every kit to: built once, stands for generations.

If you want a building that does the same — for a garage, a warehouse, a barndominium, a hangar — request a free quote or call us at 1-800-444-2525. We'll send a sealed engineering package, a fixed price, and a real ship date.

Frequently asked questions

The questions Bell engineers and project managers hear most often, answered. Don't see yours? Ask us directly.

The most common Bell builds are 30×40 garages and 60×120 warehouses. Use the quote wizard with your intended use (vehicles, equipment, livestock, retail floor) and we'll back-calculate the right footprint and clear-span height with you on a call. Smaller is rarely a mistake; under-sizing eave height is the most common regret we hear.
Bell provides stamped engineering drawings signed by a P.E. licensed in your state. You or your contractor pull the local permit using our drawings as the engineering basis. We do not pull permits directly because municipal processes vary too much for us to manage from Pennsylvania — but our drawings are the document your inspector wants to see.
Typical lead time is 6–10 weeks from signed drawings, depending on building size, finish color, and current factory queue. Your project manager gives you a hard ship date in writing. Clearance buildings ship in 7–14 days.
Bell ships completed kits to all 48 contiguous states via certified carriers. Alaska, Hawaii, and Canadian provinces are quoted case-by-case — shipping is a meaningful share of those quotes, and we want to be honest about it.
Every kit ships with: primary I-beam frame welded and drilled, secondary girts/purlins/eave struts, wall and roof panels pre-cut and pre-coated, all fasteners, trim, flashing, full assembly drawings, and the P.E.-stamped engineering package. Concrete slab, anchors, doors, and windows are owner-supplied unless specified.
Yes — but engineer it now even if you install later. We'll pre-frame openings and reinforce roof framing for a future mezzanine at design time. Retro-fitting a structural change to a coated wall is significantly harder than building the reinforcement in.
14 standard colors — 13 panel options and 6 trim options — all with the 40-year limited paint warranty. We can also color-match for an upcharge. See the full swatch chart.
40-year limited rust-through warranty on galvanized panels, 40-year limited paint warranty, 50-year limited structural warranty on the primary frame. Full terms are on the warranty page.
Yes. Bell partners with two third-party financing providers: a rent-to-own program for residential garages and small storage, and a commercial term-loan program for buildings over $25,000 delivered. financing Production: confirm partners.
Every kit is custom-engineered to your local wind, snow, seismic, and exposure category. You tell us the address, we run the IBC load calc against the latest ASCE 7. We can certify to higher loads than code minimum on request.
Yes — we keep an informal list of vetted erection crews in each state we ship to. We don't sub-contract erection ourselves (it'd cost you more and slow the schedule), but we can connect you with a crew that has built Bell kits before.
Most Bell garages and shops sit on a 4–6 inch poured concrete slab with a perimeter footing below frost line. Bigger or heavier-loaded buildings (industrial, hangar, equestrian) often warrant pier-and-grade-beam or pile foundations. Your stamped drawings include foundation reactions; your local soils engineer designs the foundation against them.

Bell's warranty, in plain language

Profile 01 · Paint

40-year limited paint warranty

Bell warrants that the silicone-polyester topcoat applied to standard galvanized panels and trim will not chalk in excess of ASTM D4214 No. 8 rating, will not fade more than 5 ΔE Hunter units (excluding white), and will not check, crack, or peel for a period of 40 years from delivery, subject to the standard exclusions below.

What's excluded: coastal exposure within 1,500 ft of salt water (separate marine warranty available), buildings within 1 mile of an active industrial emitter, hand-painted modifications, and damage from impact, fire, or improper cleaning agents.

Profile 02 · Rust-through

40-year limited rust-through warranty

Bell warrants its standard G90 galvanized substrate against perforation by corrosion for 40 years from delivery. If a covered panel or trim piece develops a corrosion-induced hole within the warranty period, Bell will furnish a replacement piece at no cost to the original purchaser. Installation labor is the owner's responsibility.

What's excluded: the same coastal / industrial / impact exclusions as above, plus damage caused by improper anchoring to dissimilar metals (galvanic corrosion at flashing-to-roofing junctions, for example).

Profile 03 · Structural

50-year limited structural warranty

Bell warrants the welded I-beam primary frame against structural failure under design-load conditions for 50 years from delivery. "Design-load conditions" means the wind, snow, seismic, and live-load values printed on your stamped engineering package — that's why we ship a copy with every kit and keep a copy on file forever.

What's excluded: loads exceeding design (a tornado on a building rated for 90 mph, for example, is not a warranty event), unpermitted owner modifications to the frame, fire, flood, and acts of war.

How to file a claim: call your Bell project manager, or call 1-800-444-2525 and ask for warranty. We'll ask for the original engineering package number (printed on every kit's drawing set), photos of the affected area, and the approximate delivery date. Most claims are decided within 10 business days.

Our story

Bell Industrial Construction was founded in 2006 in Lakeland, Florida. What started as a two-person operation—designing and building metal structures for local cattle ranchers and equipment dealers—has grown into a team of engineers, fabricators, and installation specialists serving fourteen counties across Florida's Gulf Coast. Every building we've completed since then carries the same principle: know your customer, know the land, build it right the first time.

We've engineered and installed over 1,400 metal buildings for ranchers, hay producers, equestrian operators, contractors, and business owners. We understand Florida's terrain—the sandy soils, the seasonal humidity, the hurricanes. We've watched barns survive Idalia, Irma, and the storms before that. We've learned what works when it matters.

The relationship with our customers is our business. A rancher calls with a problem in July, we're on the property in August with a quote and a timeline. An existing customer needs an addition, we know where to find them. A contractor wants sealed drawings and a guarantee, we deliver both. That's the heritage we've built.

How we work

We design, engineer, build, and install—all in-house. We're NFBA members and hold Florida General Contractors License # CGC-XXXXXX. Every quote we send includes sealed engineering drawings drawn to Florida Building Code standards. Every component meets Florida Product Approval. We don't subcontract the engineering or the installation; we do the work ourselves, so when a customer calls with a question, someone who built it answers.

Our process is straightforward: you describe your build, we return a sealed engineering quote within five business days. Florida-licensed engineers draw site-specific plans. We deliver pre-fabricated steel on a single truck. Our crews in Lakeland, Sarasota, and Fort Myers install on schedule—most buildings raise in five to fourteen days. That's how we've kept customers coming back for twenty years.

Where we work

We serve fourteen counties across Florida's west coast, roughly thirty miles north and south of our Lakeland headquarters. That's the terrain we know: flat to gently rolling, water table within a few feet of the surface, hurricanes every few years, high heat and humidity. We understand the permitting landscape in each county. We know the soils. We build for the weather.

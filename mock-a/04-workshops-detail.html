Steel workshop kits

A "kit" is everything you need to put up your workshop's shell, delivered on one truck (or two for larger buildings), with every piece labeled to the erection drawings.

Included: primary frame (columns, rafters), secondary frame (purlins, girts), roof and wall panels in the color you spec, every trim piece, every fastener, all sealants, doors and windows you've quoted, and the stamped engineering package.

Not included: the foundation, anchor bolts (we ship anchor-bolt drawings; your foundation contractor pours and embeds), electrical and plumbing rough-in, insulation if you didn't add it, permits, and labor.

Advantages of metal workshops over traditional

Three places steel pulls ahead of stick-built and pole-barn alternatives.

Value

A steel kit costs 15–25% less than the equivalent stick-built shell, depending on your zip and the spec. The bigger the span, the more steel pulls ahead.

Versatility

Clear-span design means no interior columns. Move the doors. Add a mezzanine. Reconfigure the layout in five years without touching the frame.

Safety & convenience

Non-combustible primary frame. Resistant to termites, mold, and rot. Same kit, same envelope, same insurance discount whether you're storing classic cars or a Class A motorhome.

Workshop kit FAQs

What's the smallest workshop you'll engineer?
+
Our standard catalog starts at 12×20 for a single-bay workshop. Anything smaller is quoted custom.
Can I add a lean-to or shed-roof addition?
+
Yes. Most workshop kits can accept a lean-to extension on one or both sidewalls. We engineer the connection and ship a matched roof slope.
What's the typical lead time from order to delivery?
+
Residential workshop kits: 6–8 weeks from final engineering. Commercial kits: 8–10 weeks. Clearance buildings: 5–10 business days.
Will the kit fit my existing foundation?
+
If you have an existing slab, we can engineer the kit to match your footprint — within reason. Send us slab dimensions and a photo and we'll tell you what's possible.
Do you supply the overhead doors?
+
Yes — we offer Wayne Dalton and CHI overhead doors as line items on the kit. Specify size, panel style, color, opener type, and insulation R-value.
Can I get the building in a custom color?
+
We offer 14 standard colors with 40-year paint warranty. Custom colors are available on order — minimum quantity and lead-time apply.
Will it work in my climate?
+
Bell workshops are engineered to your zip code's snow, wind, and seismic loads. From Florida hurricane zones to Wyoming high-snow country, we adjust the steel weight accordingly.
Can I store hazardous materials inside?
+
Steel envelopes are well-suited for fuel and chemical storage thanks to their non-combustible frame. Check your local code for specific containment, ventilation, and fire-rating requirements.
How long does the kit last?
+
Bell workshop kits carry a 50-year rust-perforation warranty and a 40-year paint warranty. Most installed Bell buildings outlast their original owners.
Can I install it myself?
+
Most owners with one or two helpers can install our workshop kits in 2–4 weekends. Larger workshops (40×60+) benefit from a small crew with a forklift or scissor lift.
What if I run into a problem during installation?
+
Every kit comes with a dedicated project manager who answers calls during install. We've talked customers through every bolt pattern over FaceTime more times than we can count.

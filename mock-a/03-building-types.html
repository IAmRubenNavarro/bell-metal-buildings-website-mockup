Frequently asked — building selection

How do I pick between a straight-wall building and a Quonset hut?
+
Straight-wall is best when you want vertical walls for shelving, doors, or windows. Quonset is best when raw cost-per-square-foot matters most and the arch shape doesn't get in your way.
Can I combine two building types in one kit?
+
Yes — garage+carport combos and workshop+storage combos are common. We engineer the shared wall and roof transition as one kit.
What's the largest building Bell will engineer?
+
Our standard commercial program runs up to 200 feet clear-span and 40-foot eave heights. Larger projects are quoted on a custom engineering basis.
Can I see drawings before I commit?
+
Every Bell quote comes with a preliminary set of drawings (footprint, elevations, accessory locations) so you can verify everything before signing.
Do you ship to my state?
+
We ship to all 48 contiguous states. AK, HI, and Canada are quoted case-by-case.
What's the difference between a kit price and an "installed" price?
+
Bell sells the kit (steel + drawings + accessories). Installation is sold separately and varies by region; we'll connect you with a vetted regional erector if you need one.
How long is a typical Bell quote good for?
+
Steel quotes are firm for 30 days. After that we re-pull current mill pricing — usually a small change, but we'd rather requote than surprise you.
Do you offer doors and windows as part of the kit?
+
Yes. Overhead doors, walk doors, windows, ridge vents, skylights, gutters, and insulation are all available as line items on the same engineering package.
Can I add to my building later?
+
Yes. Most Bell buildings can be lengthened by adding bays. We engineer the addition to match the original kit's loads and finishes.
Do you supply foundation drawings?
+
Yes — anchor-bolt layout and reaction loads come with every engineering package. Your local foundation contractor uses those numbers to spec the slab.

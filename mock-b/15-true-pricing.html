How Bell prices a building

Every Bell quote is an engineered estimate, not a sticker price. That means three things: the price you see is what you pay; the price reflects your specific county's snow, wind, and seismic loads; and the price is held for 30 days against global steel-market volatility.

If you've gotten a quote elsewhere that's much lower than Bell's, it's usually one of two things: either the building isn't engineered to your county's actual loads (which surfaces during permitting), or the quote is missing line items (anchor bolts, freight, sealed drawings) that will be billed later. We don't do either.

What determines my price

  1. 1

    Building size & clear-span

    Wider/longer clear-spans need heavier primary framing, which drives steel-by-weight cost. A 30×40×10 garage costs roughly half of a 40×60×14 workshop because the primary frame is half the weight.

  2. 2

    Snow, wind & seismic loads

    Your county's loads drive the engineering. A high-wind coastal site (140 mph design wind) costs more than a 90 mph inland site. A 60 psf snow load costs more than a 20 psf one.

  3. 3

    Doors, windows & openings

    Every opening needs reinforced framing. Two roll-ups cost more than one — but cheaper than adding one after the panels are on. Decide before contract.

  4. 4

    Insulation & finish package

    R-value, wainscot, color, trim, and ridge vent options all live here. Insulation is the line item most people over-spec; we'll tell you what your climate zone actually needs.

  5. 5

    Current steel market

    Bell tracks the hot-rolled-coil index daily. We hold your quote at the index price on contract day, even if it moves up afterward. If it moves down significantly, we re-issue.

Why we don't publish "starting at" prices

The "starting at $X" trick is one of the worst things about online steel-building shopping. A "starting at $9,900" two-car garage advertised on the internet is almost always missing freight, anchor bolts, sealed drawings, or any code-specific engineering — and the number you actually sign at is double. We'd rather you get a real quote in five minutes from a real human than waste your time on a teaser.

How financing fits in

We don't lend, but we work with two financing partners who specialize in agricultural and commercial steel buildings. They'll review the Bell engineered quote and offer terms — typically 5–10 year notes, 10–20% down.

What's never in your quote

Foundation work (Bell doesn't pour concrete). Permitting fees (paid to your county or state). Site grading or excavation. Crane/equipment rental for erection if you hire a crew. Local sales tax. We'll flag all of these in writing on every quote so there are no surprises.

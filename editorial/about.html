Our story

Bell Metal Buildings was founded in 2006 in Lakeland, Florida, with a straightforward promise: engineer buildings that outlast the work they shelter. Our founders knew that a barn raising is not just about steel and fasteners—it's about understanding the land, respecting the weather, and building something that will stand for generations without becoming someone else's problem.

We have since designed and installed over 1,400 metal buildings across Florida's west coast. A cattle ranch in Polk County, a warehouse on the I-75 corridor, an equestrian arena in Marion County, a mini-storage complex in Sarasota—each one has been sealed by Florida-licensed engineers and raised by our crews who show up on time, work through the heat, and leave the site clean.

We remain family-owned and staff-driven. Our Lakeland headquarters houses our design and engineering team. Our field crews operate out of Sarasota and Fort Myers. We are members of the National Frame Builders Association and maintain an A+ rating with the Better Business Bureau. We don't chase every job. We chase the ones we can engineer right.

What we do

  • Design and engineer site-specific metal buildings using Florida Building Code standards and sealed by Florida-licensed PE.
  • Fabricate and deliver pre-cut steel on schedule—galvanized frames, Galvalume AZ55 panels, and coastal-rated fasteners for salt-air jobs.
  • Install in-house through our Lakeland, Sarasota, and Fort Myers crews. Most raises complete in 5–14 days.

Service area

We serve 14 west-coast Florida counties: Citrus, Hernando, Pasco, Pinellas, Hillsborough, Polk, Manatee, Sarasota, Hardee, DeSoto, Charlotte, Lee, Collier, and Hendry. Approximately 30-mile travel radius from our Lakeland headquarters.

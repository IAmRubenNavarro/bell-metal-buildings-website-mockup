Every Decision That Shapes Your Building.

Frame gauge, panel color, door type, window count, insulation — change anything, and the price updates instantly in the 3D Designer.

Frame & gauge

14-gauge is standard. 12-gauge upgrades the columns and rafters for snow and wind — required in coastal and snow-load zones.

★

14-gauge standard

Best for most non-coastal, low-snow regions. 20-year warranty included.

★

12-gauge upgrade

Heavier columns and rafters. Required in zones rated 110+ mph wind or 30+ psf snow load.

★

Engineered drawings

Stamped drawings included on every order — your permit officer can issue from them directly.

Panels

29-gauge Galvalume is our standard. 26-gauge upgrades panel thickness for storm zones. Both come in 13 colors.

  • 29-gauge standardGalvalume coating; 20-year rust-through warranty.
  • 26-gauge upgradeStiffer panel for storm and snow zones.
  • R-panel profileVertical rib pattern, our most popular look.

Doors

Roll-up garage doors, walk-in doors, sliding barn doors. Mix and match — every door cuts a clean opening.

  • Roll-up doors6×6 up to 14×14 in standard sizes; custom on request.
  • Walk-in doors36×80 fiberglass with deadbolt, optional half-glass.
  • Sliding barn doorsFor barn and workshop styles; gable-end mount.

Windows

Single-hung windows, glass-block, or pre-framed openings if you want to install your own.

Trim & flashing

Color-matched trim on corners, eaves, gables, and ridges. We don't charge extra for color matching — it's just how we ship.

Insulation

Single-bubble, double-bubble, or rigid foam. We don't sell insulation we wouldn't put on our own building.

★

Single-bubble

Reflects radiant heat; great for carports and uninsulated workshops.

★

Double-bubble

Adds a vapor barrier and dampens roof noise.

★

Rigid foam (R-13/R-19)

For conditioned workshops and shops. Drywall-ready on request.

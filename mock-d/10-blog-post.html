How to build a steel building in 5 easy steps

By Kevin Bell · Published May 2026

Most of our customers are first-time builders. They've never poured a slab, never set a wedge anchor, never raised a column. And by the end of day one, they're standing in their own building. Here's how it works.

Step 1 — Site prep

Pick a flat, well-draining spot. A 30×40 garage needs roughly 36×46 of cleared, level ground. You can install on a poured slab, on prepared gravel, or directly on the dirt — but a slab gives you the longest service life and the best anchor purchase.

Step 2 — Anchor patterns

We provide stamped anchor drawings with every order. For slab installs we use wedge anchors. For dirt or gravel we use 30-inch mobile-home anchors or 36-inch rebar. Coastal customers should upgrade to engineered helical anchors — your permit office may require it.

Step 3 — Raise the frame

The columns set first, then the rafters drop into place. A two-person crew can raise a 24×30 garage frame in about three hours. For larger buildings we send a four-person crew with a small lift.

Step 4 — Set the panels

Roof first, then walls. Self-drilling color-matched screws make this fast. Roll-up doors and walk-in doors hang last. Trim caps off the corners, eaves, and ridge to seal everything against weather.

Step 5 — Sign off

Our crew walks the building with you. Anchor count, door operation, panel alignment — we want you to confirm every detail before we leave. Once you sign, we send your warranty packet and we're gone.
